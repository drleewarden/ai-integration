# Data Safety Checklist for AI Tools

**Creative Milk — Members Library · v1 (July 2026)**

Use this checklist twice: once when evaluating a new AI tool, and once when writing the rules your team follows every day. It pairs with the AI Usage Policy Template in the members library.

> This checklist is general guidance for Australian small businesses, not legal advice. If you handle health, financial or children's data, get specific advice for your obligations.

---

## Part 1 — Before you adopt any AI tool (vendor checks)

Tick every box before the tool touches real business data.

- [ ] **Training use.** Does the vendor use your inputs to train their models? Look for a plain statement, and for business plans that turn it off by default. If you can't find the answer in ten minutes, that is the answer.
- [ ] **Data retention.** How long are your prompts and files kept? Can you delete them? Is there a retention setting you control?
- [ ] **Where the data lives.** Which country hosts the data? If you have customers in the EU/UK or contracts that require Australian hosting, confirm the tool can comply.
- [ ] **Access within the vendor.** Can vendor staff read your data? Under what circumstances (support, abuse review)?
- [ ] **Business vs consumer plan.** Consumer (free) tiers almost always have weaker data terms than business tiers of the same product. Check the terms for the tier you'll actually use.
- [ ] **Security basics.** Does the vendor publish a security page? Do they support two-factor authentication and (if you have staff) centralised account management?
- [ ] **Exit.** Can you export your data and delete the account cleanly if you leave?

**Red flags that mean stop:** no privacy policy or one that hasn't been updated in years · training on your data with no opt-out · no way to delete your data · the tool is free with no visible business model (you are the product).

## Part 2 — Everyday rules for you and your team

Print this section. Stick it near the desks.

### Never paste into an AI tool

- [ ] Passwords, API keys, or anything that grants access to a system
- [ ] Full payment card numbers or bank login details — ever, in any tool
- [ ] Tax file numbers, Medicare numbers, passport or licence numbers
- [ ] Health information about an identifiable person
- [ ] Anything covered by a client confidentiality agreement, unless the tool is approved for it in writing

### De-identify before pasting

- [ ] Customer or staff names → "the customer", "a team member"
- [ ] Email addresses and phone numbers → remove or replace with placeholders
- [ ] Addresses → suburb-level only, if location matters at all
- [ ] Unique details that identify someone even without a name (e.g. "our only client in Ballarat")

### Fine to use freely

- [ ] Your own marketing copy, website text and public documents
- [ ] Generic business questions and drafting ("write a follow-up email for a quote")
- [ ] Aggregated numbers that don't identify anyone ("revenue grew 12%")
- [ ] Anything already published

---

## Part 3 — Set the house rules (15 minutes, once)

- [ ] Write down the **approved tools list** — which AI tools the business uses, on which plan, and for what. Three lines per tool is plenty.
- [ ] Name **one owner** — the person who approves new tools and reviews the list twice a year.
- [ ] Agree the **de-identify rule** above with everyone who uses AI tools, including contractors.
- [ ] Add the **payment-details rule**: any change to bank or payment details, however it arrives, is verified by phone on a number you already had. AI-written scam emails no longer contain typos.
- [ ] Decide **what happens on a mistake** — if someone pastes something they shouldn't have: tell the owner same-day, delete the conversation/thread if the tool allows it, and note what happened. No blame; the goal is that people report fast.

## Quick reference card

| Situation | Rule |
|---|---|
| New AI tool looks useful | Part 1 checks first, then owner approves |
| Drafting with client context | De-identify names and contact details |
| Anything with credentials or card numbers | Never |
| Email asks to change payment details | Phone verification, known number |
| Pasted something you shouldn't have | Tell the owner today, delete the thread |
